Planetary Geological Mapping

Lets start with what geological mapping is! Geological mapping is the creation of a special map made to display various geological features. These special maps represent the distribution of different types of rock and deposits, as well as locations of geological structures such as faults and folds. It is a highly interpretive, scientific process that can produce a range of map products for many different uses, including assessing ground water quality and contamination risks; predicting earthquake, volcano, and landslide hazards.
Now Lets apply this knowledge to the planetary objects! The goal of geological mapping is to place observations of surface features into stratigraphic context in order to develop generalized geologic timescales that provide a sequence of events fore the evolution of planetary surfaces. Geological maps are tools that have aided in the understanding of the process that produced morphologically-distinct materials observed throughout the Solar System, and that chemical and physical analysis of a given site on a planetary body can be investigated far better if the geological setting of the site is known first. Selecting a position on the planet to land a rover will be far less risky knowing the geological site first.
